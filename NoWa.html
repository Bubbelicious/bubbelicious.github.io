NoWa - No Waste

This school project was a group assignment where we
were told to focus on a subject within sustainable living. After
some discussion we decided to work with sustainable food consumption
with focus on food waste at home.

Phase 1 - User research

Quantitative data was gathered with a form that we spread among our different social-groups. Data that was collected were
concentrated on both the food waste as well as cooking and shopping habits, to find out where in the process we should focus
our effort.

Quality data was gathered with the use of both spontaneous short interviews with customers outside a food market, as well as a
few slightly more in-depth interviews with people in our social group.

With the help of an affinity diagram we analysed the data and reached some preliminary conclusions.

Planning your food shopping around your meals should lessen the food waste, and help the ones that
are unstructural to be proactive.

When you have less money, you plan your shopping better which makes for less food waste.

Result of research

We learned that when it comes to shopping, people tend to shop for food 2-3 times/week and buy additions when needed,
or they shop only when they need something at random times during the week.

Majority of people have a shopping list, but tend to stray from it because of hankering and special prices, and they don’t usually
plan ahead their meals and instead cook what they feel like at the time.

What they want to have help with, according to our questions, are structure and planning around what they have at home and
when it expires.

The Problem

The average person throw out too much food because of bad planning.
We want to help them with the structure in their day to lessen their waste.

Phase 2 - Concept

We wanted our product to contribute to an easier way in planning the meals and shopping for every household.
We wanted it to have an overview of the products that are already in the household and when they expire.

But

We also need it to be easy to use and time effective, at least some degree of auto generating,
as well as being able to give cooking inspiration to the user.

Process of product design

Earlier ideas included a device that was to be attached
onto the refrigerator, and have a detachable scanner you could use to
scan every food product you wanted to keep a closer eye on.

After some discussion and brainstorming among the group,
we instead agreed to concentrate on designing an app in which all the
functions we wanted to incorporate could be added.
