NoWa - No Waste

This school project was a group assignment where we
were told to focus on a subject within sustainable living. After
some discussion we decided to work with sustainable food consumption
with focus on food waste at home.

Phase 1 - User research

Quantitative data was gathered with a form that we spread among our different social-groups. Data that was collected were
concentrated on both the food waste as well as cooking and shopping habits, to find out where in the process we should focus
our effort.

Quality data was gathered with the use of both spontaneous short interviews with customers outside a food market, as well as a
few slightly more in-depth interviews with people in our social group.

With the help of an affinity diagram we analysed the data and reached some preliminary conclusions.

Planning your food shopping around your meals should lessen the food waste, and help the ones that
are unstructural to be proactive.

When you have less money, you plan your shopping better which makes for less food waste.

Result of research

We learned that when it comes to shopping, people tend to shop for food 2-3 times/week and buy additions when needed,
or they shop only when they need something at random times during the week.

Majority of people have a shopping list, but tend to stray from it because of hankering and special prices, and they don’t usually
plan ahead their meals and instead cook what they feel like at the time.

What they want to have help with, according to our questions, are structure and planning around what they have at home and
when it expires.

The Problem

The average person throw out too much food because of bad planning.
We want to help them with the structure in their day to lessen their waste.

Phase 2 - Concept

We wanted our product to contribute to an easier way in planning the meals and shopping for every household.
We wanted it to have an overview of the products that are already in the household and when they expire.

But

We also need it to be easy to use and time effective, at least some degree of auto generating,
as well as being able to give cooking inspiration to the user.

Process of product design

Earlier ideas included a device that was to be attached
onto the refrigerator, and have a detachable scanner you could use to
scan every food product you wanted to keep a closer eye on.

After some discussion and brainstorming among the group,
we instead agreed to concentrate on designing an app in which all the
functions we wanted to incorporate could be added.

Finished Concept

Bar codes contain product names, best-before dates and expiration warnings (already available at Coop, for example,
where store staff receive expiration warnings on products, and can lower the price of products a few days before) so it
should not be so difficult to get this information in the app when scanning the code.

The purpose of our app is to reduce food waste in the home with the help of an app that keeps track of items in your
refrigerator, pantry when they are about to pass the best-before date, etc.

It assists people with inspiration by searching for recipes based on what is in the consumer's home and it uses food
that has already been prepared to in a smart and effective way reduce the risk of saved food having to be thrown away,
and help consumers with an overview in their economy.

The app helps private individuals to plan food consumption in the household (function)
by providing functions that automate shopping lists based on the menu, and provides an overview
of the food budget and all food items in the home, including the food expiration date (mechanism).
The user gets a time-efficient tool that reduces personal food waste
and thus saves the user money and eases their conscience (value).

What the customer wants to do is buy groceries, cook and eat all the food. (Functional).
Furthermore, he wants a good household economy (Emotional) and wants to be viewed as an
environmental friend, or at least not one who ignores the environment, and that they want to
be perceived as someone who has control (Social).

However, according to our primary type of behavior it is difficult to meet these goals, mainly
because they are stressed and unstructured. They often know what would need to be done,
but does not get to the structure and planning.

Customer pains

They are usually in the store without a shopping list or plan for what to cook for
the week. Purchases go badly, they buy twice as much of something that
is already at home or forgets to buy certain products because they do not know
what is already at home and what is to be cooked for the week. Food has time to
get old and is forgotten at the bottom of the fridge. They throw away food and
have a bad conscience about it as it costs money and they know that it is bad
for the environment.

Pain reliever: As our product NOWA No Waste, is a mobile app, the customer always
have all the functions at hand both at home and in the store. The app syncs the
shopping list to what you have at home, it reminds you of the expiration date for
what will soon be out and comes automatically with recipe and menu suggestions based
on these items. You can also get an overview of your food budget and know what
deposits you have made and what you have bought.

Customer Gains

What the customer would like is to save time and money, have a structure to be able
to reduce their food waste and ease their conscience. They also need inspiration for
everyday cooking, both in the form of recipes and menus.

Gain creator: The functions in the product offer an overview and help with planning.
Most things are created automatically (menu, shopping lists, recipes) so they do not
get one more thing on the "to-do list" that takes time. Everything is connected to
the store card you have at Coop, for example. If the person has used other apps
and websites that do not communicate with each other but are used one by one, or maybe
lists on paper, then this will now be an app where you have gathered
everything. It is an efficient and time-saving tool that brings together all the
functions that are about food shopping and cooking in one place.
