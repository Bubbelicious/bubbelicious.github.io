NärCon Panda

This website is a recrutement site for the big event organization NärCon.
I have personally been a volonteer at their events since 2012, and unfortunately the
website has not changed much since then, and is very outdated. Also, considdering the
current regulations about accessability and availability online, NärCon panda does not
meet the requirement that are recommended.
This is a problem since NärCon is very adamant about accepting everyone no matter
of disability or other. I have therefore taken upon myself to redesigning the site to meet
the WCAG requirement as well as updating it and adapting it with a mobile design
(something the current one lacks).

Research

Since I have personal experience with the site, not much actual research was needed to collect the data I needed.
I have heard many complaints about the site, and started noting them down when I decided that it needed to be
changed. I completed these notes with a few unstructural interviews to get the whole picture.
I used the tool Lighthouse, as well as Siteimprove Accessability Checker, to get the exact data of what needs to be
adjusted for the site to be up to WCAG requirement.

"I can never remember how to find the profile settings..."

"The private message system is driving me crazy! I always click on the wrong thing."

"Why can't I search for the things I actually need to find?"

Result of Research

From my research I learned that while the graphics are outdated, they hold up to the WCAG requirement for color and
contrast but the site is severly underworked in terms of accessability in the code. The site is not adapted for people with
sight disabilities or other who need to tab their way through the site. This is not something I can personally do anything
about, but I will bring it up none the less.

On the other hand, the personal interviews gave an entirely other perspective. The users are often frustrated with the
navigation on the site, and find it hard to locate what they are looking for.
While the navigation seems to be the biggest concern for users, they all have other problems that they mention. Some
reaccuring ones are: Private Messages, Profile settings, Applications, Search, and Discussions.

First Design

One things I had to keep in mind when redesigning the site was that NärCon has a very recognizable profile, and I
had to make sure that my design incorporated. The website was to be given a desperatly needed update, but it
still had to be recognized as one of NärCon's sites.
Tidying up the navigation was my main concern, as well as figuring out how the site would look as it adapted itself
to a mobile user. These would be the first things I worked on before going deeper into the functions on the site.

Start page

For the start page, and log in function, I tidied up the navigation to make
it easier to find what you are looking for, and moved the functions of
creating an account and forgotten password into the log in function since you don't
want to have to look for it if you need it.

While I kept the idea of a background image, I made sure to darken it enough
for it to not be distracting or annoying for the eyes.

I made sure that the design for the mobile adaption was as close to the desktop
site as possible, since it needs to still read as the same website.

I decided to make the navigation into a hamburger menu to make it as easy to
navigate as possible without making it cluttered.
