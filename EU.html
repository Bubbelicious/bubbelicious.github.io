EU- Bike

This school assignment consisted of doing user research, idea workshop, redesigning the app,
and then presenting the changes to "the client" (which in this case was played by our teacher).
Research and workshop was done in groups, while the redesigning phase was done individually.
Research Team: Maya Otterstål (me), Jessica Banefelt, Måns Järneström, Joel Öhrman

User research

The first thing we did once we got this assignment was to read the reviews from the app store, on how users thought
about using the app, and it was clear that the users were not happy with it. After testing the app ourself, we quickly
found several pain points in the user flow that should be retified. We tested the same user flows on other
people in our social surrounding and found that they had almost exactly the same problem that we had
with the app.
Using a customer journey map, we mapped the general flow and used the data we had recieved from our user testings
to fill it out, with very clear results.

Result of research

The first thing we noticed was that people are very reluctant to give out their phone number
when they don't know anything about the app yet. Many commented that they would much rather
log in with their Google-account or email. A quick check also showed that there is no information on
how you have your account, as well as any information given, erased. The Privacy Policy states that
you can have your information deleted at any time, but does not say how to go about it. This is a direct
violation against GDPR rules on collecting personal information and need to be retified as soon as possible.

Once logged in to the app, user get very confused on what to do next since there is nothing
that indicated what your next move should be. After testing out the map, and checking the menu
(which resulted in it's own frustrations) users would try out the scanning function on the closest
bike, only to find out that they needed to buy credits before scanning.
Long story short, the users became very frustrated with user flow, lack of choices both in
logging in and paying, lack of information, as well as the bad condition of the bikes that could be rented.

Workshop

Our group decided to do a workshop for idea generating. The participants were another group in our class,
and we had some issues with the actual implementation of the workshop after the outbreak of Covid-19
but eventually managaed to adjust it into a workshop online.

The workshop exercises we decided on using were, firstly a simple warm-up of writing down three positive
and three negative things about the app on post-its. Next we did an exercise called Wishful thinking,
where the participants were asked to come up with as many ideas they could that would improve the app.
The crazier the better, but simple things could also be added. Lastly we did an exercise that's called Pessimist
vs Optimist, where everyone would chose one of their ideas from the earlier exercise and defend it against the
other participants, who would try to come up with issues in the idea.

While both we as workshop leaders and the participants had some issues getting used to performing the
exercises over a video chat, we all thought it went well and that it was a fun time.
