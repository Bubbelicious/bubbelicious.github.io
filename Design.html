NoWa - No Waste

An app born from a school project, the first big project we were given to teach us how to work together as we might have to on a workplace. We were a group of five which decided to concentrate on the theme of Sustainable Food Consumption, and once the project was started, we begun with the User research. We interviewed people outside of a local food market to learn about shopping behaviour, put out a survey to learn about the food that people are throwing out, and we started observing the people in our lives and how they handled food. After analysing the data, creating personality types and outlining the need of the users, we started sketching out rough designs on paper. Different kind of products were introduced during this phase, to try and find the most useful, such as an electronic list for your fridge to keep track on expiration dates, but it was decided that an app would cover more functions that we needed. So we started writing out functions and planning the flow of the app, splitting up the tasks to cover more ground. By this stage I was nominated as design lead, and after the main design was decided upon, I was in charge of producing almost all of the later iterations that were needed between testings, including the logo we decided on. For the first design, we all chose one function each to focus on, to first make up some low-fid wireframes, and after feedback- and brainstorming sessions, we moved up to low fid prototypes on Figma. After that we did our first user testing, and made a high-fid prototype based on the data we received, with me behind the necissery re-designs, and after one last user testing and iteration with smaller changes, we presented our work to the rest of the class and our teacher. Our project received the highest grade on all deliveries.
