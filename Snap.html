Snap!

A household game to make chores into fun

The graduation project that I created for the end of my education for UX design and Requirement engineering at Chas Academy. The area that I chose to focus on was the difficulities that people with executive disabilities, that are connected to diagnoses like Autism, ADHD, etc, face every day when trying to get thing done.

"In every job that must be done there is an element of fun. You find the fun and -SNAP!- the job's a game!"
- Mary Poppins

Phase 1 - Data and Analysis

After some preliminary research into the subject of Neuropsychiatric diagnoses, I narrowed down the functions of the brain that make up most of the executive abilities that people with these kind of issues have problem with.
Motivation, Concentration, Planning, Memory and Energy seemed to be the greatest villains of this story.
Using these functions I then gathered quantitative data by sending out a form on social media to find out which of these functions that gave them the most struggle. I also gathered information about what kind of supportive products they have used before and which of these were most helpful, as well as if they have a support person in their life.

To follow up on the statistics from the form, I also encouraged people to sign up for a unstruktured intervju to help me get a better understanding of the answers behind the numbers.

With the help of an affinity diagram I then analysed the data to be able to create behaviour types that would help me keep my work on the right track.

The Executive Disabled

The Supporter
"You nag and you nag but nothing gets done, until you have had enough and do it yourself instead."

Results

I had to do two different analytics since my first form was only reached by people over the age of 26. I used the same form, but had some help (by Lena Ogland) reaching a younger age group.
The results of the two analytics had no significant difference, so age would have no impact on further work.

I found that the majority of people with executive disabilities find that Motivation and Energy are what they have the most issues with in their life, and depending on their diagnosis and age they may also experience some smaller issues with either Concentration or Memory as well.

According to the statistics they have also used a series of different products to try and help, but they find lists the most helpful so far even if it loses it's helpfulness as time passes. The statistics also said that not many had tried a rewarding system before, but all the ones that had tried it found it the most helpful thing they had ever tried.

Phase 2 - Concept and Design

To get a grip of the concept that I wanted to focus on for this project I sketched out the different stakeholders as well as their preliminary needs. It's was during this process that I realized that the app would need to have two different accounts depending on if you needed help or if you were helping.

Snap! gives people with Neuropsychiatric disabilities, young and old, the possibility to do their chores without the additional stess (function), by helping to motivate the user into doing them without having to think about it themselves. With Snap! it's easy to plan and prioritize things that need to be done, etc (mechanism). The user gains more indepenency and don't have to experience the stess/anxiety that occures when they try to get things done (value).

Phase 3 - Wireframes and Iterations

Now it is finally time for the fun part of any project: to create!
I started with creating basic wireframes to create the flow that I had already decided on in the earlier phase. Once I had them all down, I asked my mentor (Conor Souness) for his opinions and we had a discussion about navigation options and colors that helped me make decisions that I implemented.

The wireframes were then put together into a first prototype that I tested on five volontary users from earlier interviews. The feedback and information that was gathered in these test were used to iterate the wireframes before I started working on the details of the functions and the UI.

Version 2 - After Discussion

After feedback and discussion I decided on making sure that there would be several ways to go back to the starting page (drag down, close button and logo), so that all users could use it without having to think.I also pinned down the colors that I wanted to use; Orange for energy and playfulness, and Purple for peace and independence.

Version 3 - After User test

The user testings brought out some issues with the design that had to be addressed, as well as some things that I saved for later implementations. Things that I iterated on were the priority in the navigation, information about the difference in accounts, some different names and symbols, and the ability to change the order of the lists directly.

I finished up the design with colors and a font that was easy to read but fit the theme of the app.

Results

This project resulted in a functioning MVP prototyp for an app that, according to my inquiries and research, should be an assisting tool for people with executive disabilities as well as lower the amount of time and effort any support person for these people have to spend to help them. The app should reduce and/or remove the amount of Energy that is used when disabled functions such as Motivation, Planning, Concentration and Memory are needed, which should in turn reduce and/or remove the additional stress and/or anxiety.

The user flow of the app is intuitive and easy to use. The main function is that the user can list the activities and/or chores that need to be done and then recieve points once they are finished. Once enough points are collected the user level up and recieves a reward that has been decided upon beforehand.
There are two different accounts depending on if you are the one needing the help, or if you are the one helping. This is because during my research I found that the majority of the people that I talked to confessed that they needed an authority figure to approve of their job to get things done.

Insights and the Future

This has been an inspiring project to work on, and since I too have executive disabilities because of Neuropsychiatric disorders this app would be something that I myself would be vary thankful to be able to use. Many of the people that I have talked to during this project have sid that they feel the same, and they hope that the app will be available some time in the future, which makes me feel happy and accomplished since that means that I reached the goals that I was shooting for with this project.

This has been an idea that I have had for many years, and it is an amazing feeling to finally make it real, and I will continue working on it and hope that I can get it developed some day.
