Snap!

A household game to make chores into fun

The graduation project that I created for the end of my education
for UX design and Requirement engineering at Chas Academy.
The area that I chose to focus on was the difficulities that people with
executive disabilities, that are connected to diagnoses like Autism,
ADHD, etc, face every day when trying to get thing done.

Phase 1 - Data and Analysis

After some preliminary research into the subject of Neuropsychiatric diagnoses, I narrowed down the functions of the brain
that make up most of the executive abilities that people with these kind of issues have problem with.
Motivation, Concentration, Planning, Memory and Energy seemed to be the greatest villains of this story.
Using these functions I then gathered quantitative data by sending out a form on social media to find out which of these
functions that gave them the most struggle.
I also gathered information about what kind of supportive products they have used before and which of these were most
helpful, as well as if they have a support person in their life.

To follow up on the statistics from the form, I also encouraged people to sign up for a unstruktured intervju to help me get a
better understanding of the answers behind the numbers.

With the help of an affinity diagram I then analysed the data to be able to create behaviour types that would help me keep
my work on the right track.

The Executive Disabled
"It's like you don't have an On switch, you have to crank up the engine every time you want something done."

The Supporter
"You nag and you nag but nothing gets done, until you have had enough and do it yourself instead."

Results

I had to do two different analytics since my first form was only reached by people over the age of 26. I used the same form,
but had some help (by Lena Ogland) reaching a younger age group.
The results of the two analytics had no significant difference, so age would have no impact on further work.

I found that the majority of people with executive disabilities find that Motivation and Energy are what they have the
most issues with in their life, and depending on their diagnosis and age they may also experience some smaller issues
with either Concentration or Memory as well.

According to the statistics they have also used a series of different products to try and help, but they find lists the most
helpful so far even if it loses it's helpfulness as time passes. The statistics also said that not many had tried a rewarding
system before, but all the ones that had tried it found it the most helpful thing they had ever tried.

Phase 2 - Concept and Design

To get a grip of the concept that I wanted to focus on for this project I
sketched out the different stakeholders as well as their preliminary
needs. It's was during this process that I realized that the app would
need to have two different accounts depending on if you needed help or
if you were helping.

Snap! gives people with Neuropsychiatric disabilities, young and old, the possibility to do their
chores without the additional stess (function), by helping to motivate
the user into doing them without having to think about it themselves. With Snap! it's
easy to plan and prioritize things that need to be done, etc (mechanism).
The user gains more indepenency and don't have to experience the stess/anxiety
that occures when they try to get things done (value).
